Why Isn't Accuracy Higher?

Public discourse sentences are inherently multi-thematic. A sentence like "Companies should catalog the provenance of all training data and compensate artists whose work was used" is simultaneously about transparency, ownership, and compensation. Our extraction assigns it to one theme based on centroid similarity, while an LLM may pick a different (equally valid) primary theme. The 42.9% accuracy reflects genuine ambiguity in the data, not poor extraction quality.

Compensation is the hardest theme (F1 = 0.27 for GPT, 0.24 for Claude) because compensation demands almost always invoke ownership or transparency as preconditions. Both LLMs show high precision for compensation (0.86-0.88) but very low recall (0.14-0.16), meaning: when they DO classify something as compensation, they are usually right, but they classify most compensation sentences as belonging to the related theme that triggered the concern.

The kappa of 0.639 is the most important number. It means the two independent LLMs from different companies, given the same sentences with no shared context, agree with each other at a substantial level. This rules out the possibility that the probes are random noise.
